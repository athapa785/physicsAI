# Circular Motion and Rotational Dynamics

## Key Concepts
- Uniform circular motion
- Centripetal acceleration and force
- Angular velocity and acceleration
- Moment of inertia
- Torque and rotational equilibrium

## Mathematical Formulas
- Centripetal acceleration: \(a_c = \frac{v^2}{r} = \omega^2r\)
- Centripetal force: \(F_c = \frac{mv^2}{r} = m\omega^2r\)
- Angular velocity: \(\omega = \frac{2\pi}{T} = \frac{v}{r}\)
- Torque: \(\tau = r F \sin\theta\)
- Rotational kinetic energy: \(K = \frac{1}{2}I\omega^2\)

## Example
A car travels around a circular track of radius 100m at a constant speed of 20 m/s.
Find:
a) The centripetal acceleration
b) The centripetal force if the car's mass is 1000 kg

Solution:
1. Centripetal acceleration:
   \(a_c = \frac{v^2}{r} = \frac{(20)^2}{100} = 4\text{ m/s}^2\)

2. Centripetal force:
   \(F_c = ma_c = 1000(4) = 4000\text{ N}\)
